Discover the Heart of America:

Take an Unforgettable Road Trip from New York to Salt Lake City!

Join Us on a Journey Through the Heart of America!

Buckle up and get ready to explore the heart of America with us as we drive from New York to Salt Lake City! Our route will take us through ten incredible states: New York, Pennsylvania, Ohio, Indiana, Illinois, Missouri, Iowa, Nebraska, Wyoming, and Utah. With each state offering its unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty, this trip promises to be a feast for the senses. From the rugged terrain of Wyoming to the rolling hills of Pennsylvania, we'll be treated to stunning landscapes that will take our breath away. Along the way, we'll encounter friendly locals, vibrant communities, and charming towns that are sure to capture our hearts. We'll also have plenty of opportunities to indulge in local cuisine, visit historical sites, and take part in outdoor activities that will keep us energized and engaged. So, if you're up for an epic road trip adventure that will take you through some of America's most iconic landmarks and hidden gems, then join us on this unforgettable journey from New York to Salt Lake City!

Day 1: New York to Harmony to Rochester

We'll start our trip early in the morning and drive from New York to Harmony, Pennsylvania, which is approximately 5 hours away. Harmony is an important site for LDS history, as it is where Joseph Smith and his wife Emma lived when they first began translating the Book of Mormon. We can visit the Susquehanna River, where Joseph Smith received many of his early revelations, and where he first met Oliver Cowdery. We can also visit the Isaac Hale Home and the Priesthood Restoration Site. After exploring Harmony, we'll drive to Rochester, New York, which is approximately 2 hours away. In Rochester, we can rest for the night before starting our exploration of other LDS historic sites in the area.

Day 2: Visit Palmyra and Fayette

On the second day of our trip, we'll start early and drive to Palmyra, New York, which is about an hour away from Rochester. Palmyra is the birthplace of the LDS church and is home to many important historic sites, including the Sacred Grove where Joseph Smith had his first vision, the Hill Cumorah where he received the golden plates, and the Palmyra Temple. Spend the morning visiting these historic sites before heading to Fayette, New York, which is about 30 minutes away. In Fayette, we can visit the Peter Whitmer Farm, which is where the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints was officially organized on April 6, 1830. After a full day of exploring, we'll return to Rochester to rest for the night.

Day 3: Visit Niagara Falls

On the third day of our trip, we'll drive to Niagara Falls, New York, which is approximately 1.5 hours away from Rochester. Niagara Falls is one of the most spectacular natural wonders in the world and is a must-see for anyone visiting the area. Spend the day exploring the falls and its attractions, which include boat tours, hiking trails, and scenic overlooks. After a full day of adventure, we'll drive back to Rochester to rest for the night.

Day 4: Rochester to Cleveland

On the fourth day of our trip, we'll drive from Rochester to Kirtland, Ohio, which is approximately 4 hours away. Kirtland was an important center of LDS activity in the early 19th century and is home to many historic sites, including the Kirtland Temple, the John Johnson Farm, and the Newel K. Whitney Store. Spend the afternoon exploring these historic sites before driving to Cleveland, Ohio, which is about 30 minutes away. In Cleveland, we can rest for the night before continuing our journey.

Day 5: Visit Kirtland

On the fifth day of our trip, we'll spend the day exploring the historic sites in Kirtland. Start with a visit to the Kirtland Temple, which is the first temple built by the LDS church. Next, visit the John Johnson Farm, which is where Joseph Smith and his family lived for a time in Kirtland. Finally, visit the Newel K. Whitney Store, which is where many important church meetings were held. After a full day of exploring, we'll drive back to Cleveland to rest for the night.

Day 6: Kirtland to Nauvoo

Upon arriving in Nauvoo, we can explore the historic downtown area and visit the Joseph Smith Historic Site. This site includes several buildings that played a significant role in LDS history, such as the Red Brick Store and the Mansion House. We can also visit the Nauvoo Temple, which was rebuilt by the LDS church in the late 20th century. We'll rest for the night in Nauvoo.

Day 7: Nauvoo to Winter Quarters

On the seventh day of our trip, we'll spend the morning exploring more of Nauvoo's historic sites before driving to Winter Quarters, Nebraska, which is approximately 8 hours away. Winter Quarters was a temporary settlement established by the LDS church in the mid-19th century, and it served as a gathering place for pioneers preparing to journey westward. We can visit the Winter Quarters Visitor Center and the Mormon Pioneer Cemetery before resting for the night.

Day 8: Winter Quarters to Cheyenne

On the eighth day of our trip, we'll continue our journey westward and drive to Cheyenne, Wyoming, which is approximately 8 hours away. Cheyenne is the capital of Wyoming and is home to several historic sites, such as the Wyoming State Museum and the Cheyenne Depot Museum. We'll rest for the night in Cheyenne.

Day 9: Cheyenne to Salt Lake City

On the ninth day of our trip, we'll drive from Cheyenne to Salt Lake City, Utah, which is approximately 8 hours away. Salt Lake City is the headquarters of the LDS church and is home to many important historic sites, such as Temple Square, the Salt Lake Temple, and the Church History Museum. We'll rest for the night in Salt Lake City.

Day 10, 11: Visit Salt Lake City and Temple Square

On the tenth and eleventh days of our trip, we'll spend two full days exploring Salt Lake City and Temple Square. We can visit the Tabernacle, the Conference Center, and the Family History Library, among other sites. There are also several museums in the area, such as the Pioneer Memorial Museum and the Natural History Museum of Utah. We'll rest for the night in Salt Lake City.

Day 12: Visit Yellow Stone

On the twelfth day of our trip, we'll drive to Yellowstone National Park, which is approximately 4 hours away from Salt Lake City. Yellowstone is one of the most beautiful national parks in the United States and is home to many natural wonders, such as geysers, hot springs, and wildlife. We can spend the day exploring the park before driving back to Salt Lake City to rest for the night.

Day 13: Visit BYU Idaho

On the thirteenth day of our trip, we'll drive from Salt Lake City to BYU Idaho in Rexburg, Idaho, which is approximately 3.5 hours away. BYU Idaho is a private university owned by the LDS church, and it is home to several historic sites, such as the Jacob Spori Building and the Thomas E. Ricks Building. We can also visit the campus's beautiful gardens and outdoor areas. After exploring the university, we'll drive back to Salt Lake City to rest for the night.

Day 14: Head back to New York

On the final day of our trip, we'll start early in the morning and begin driving back to New York, which is a journey of approximately 34 hours. We can stop at hotels along the way to rest overnight.
